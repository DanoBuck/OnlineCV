DDSL Continued

I became a football referee in 2012 when I was a young 17 year old. I was in my final year of secondary school and needed some source of weekly income. I had played football all my life and it made sense to me to become a referee. This way I was killing two birds with one stone. I love watching football and getting paid for it was even better.


I refereed football games mainly on Saturday and Sunday mornings although sometimes I was lucky enough to take charge of mid-week games. I first started off in the 7-aside tier of the DDSL. Theses matches were aged from 7 - 11 year olds. I refereed these age groups for a year and was fortunate enough to take charge of a final during the close of the season. This was a great achievement as I was not even at it a year.


After this year was up, I started being given more difficult matches. These were mainly between 12 - 16 year olds. At that time I was 18 so I was not far off of them age wise. It was a nervous start but after getting some experience of games behind me I found it a lot easier. From time to time it was difficult but this was always expected. I found these challenges made me better as a referee.


In my last season, I was refereeing all ages in the game. All the way from 12 up to 18. I also entered another league which is called the UCFL. This league was for senior players. It was challenging at times but was a great experience to be a part of it.


Unfortunately, my career came to a close once I started working full-time. Working a 5-day week and trying to referee on the weekend was very tough. I stuck with it for two months before I decided to call it a day. It was a great experience and a great profession to be part of. I met wonderful people every week that were all passionate about the beautiful game.

Penalty Points in Ireland

Project Summary

To complete this project, I gained data from the Road Safety Authority. I got all the data of types of offences and how many penalty points were issued for a particular month for those offences. The data contained all statistics for each county in the Republic of Ireland.


When I first gained the data it was in a real mess. I was not able to import it straight into Tableau. I needed to clean up the data. To do this I wrote a series of scripts in C# to help me to clean this. The script was hard work but after some time well spent I had a much better way of cleaning it rather than trying to do it manually. After these scripts were ran, the data was now ready to be imported into Tableau.


After this, I made my visualisations which can be seen at the top of this section. These visualisations show the offences by counties on a map. Each county is broken down into a percentage to give the user a better idea of what counties are worst. The worst the county is, the darker shade of blue it gets. The bar chart underneath the map shows the penalty point break down by offence and county. This is linked to the map and filters accordingly with what is selected.


All the code and visualisations live in a repository on GitHub. These can be found at here.

Online Appointments for Public Services Cards

Project Summary

This project was built over 6 weeks in spare time after work in ASP.NET MVC C#. This was part of my college work while out in industry on internship.


The project had three main users, managers, staff members, and customers. However there is another user but this user is not registered and can only see the basic views i.e. the home page which shows information about the system, office locations represented on a map, and the about page.


This project was developed on my own. It was a mock appointment system. This allowed the user to make a new profile on the system. Then the customer had the ability to book an appointment of their choosing, including where and when with us, as long as this time and place was not booked up already. For simplicity, I allowed the customer to pick the member of staff in which they wanted to attend an appointment with. After the user confirmed the appointment, they received an email from the system with the appointment information.


The user also had the ability to amend their booking. This process was done similarly to the creation of the appointment.


Once a booking was made with a member of staff, the staff members appointment would appear on their view once they logged in. The staff member had the ability to cancel the appointment as well as the customer. An email was also generated in this case.


Managers were the only users who could add a new staff member, this would also create the staff members profile and generate an email to them containing their credentials. Managers also could see all their colleagues appointments in a table format. A chart would appear on the managers view showing what time is the busiest. Managers could also add new offices.


Unfortunately, my free 30 day trial with Microsoft Azure is up. Therefore my system is not being hosted anymore. However, my source code and documentation are living on GitHub. My repository can be found at here.
